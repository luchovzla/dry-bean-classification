Why Beans?



Through-out the world, beans are a mainstay for many diets. They are easy to grow, store, and prepare while also being inexpensive. Beans come in many varieties. They are packed with protein and fiber and are low in fat and calories. They are also exceptionally high in a wide variety of vitamins and minerals.

The graphs below provide a high-level description of the beans used in this data set.

The vertical bar graph identifies the 7 types of beans used in this data set and categorizes them based on their average perimeter, area, and convex area (in pixels). You will see that the Bombay bean has the highest value in all three categories. [See picture above]

The horizontal bar graph identifies the beans by the number represented in the data set. The bean with the highest frequency (3,546) was the Dermason bean.
